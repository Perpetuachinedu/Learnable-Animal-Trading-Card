The Homing Pigeon

This special breed of dove, called the rock dove, can cover a distance of about 1800 km at the sped of 97km/h. However, in 2019, a new world record was set in netherlands for the fastest racing pigeon flight, distance flown 239km at speed above 143 km/h

The homing pigeon, also called the mail pigeon or messenger pigeon, is a variety of domestic pigeons derived from the wild rock dove, selectively bred for its ability to find its way home over extremely long distances. In recent times, the homing pigeon has been used in the IT company to carry a data packed 4 GB memory stick agaisnt the ADSL service provider, Telcom, over a distance of 80km (50miles). With this pigeon, the data transfer took 2hrs, 6mins, and 57 sec - the same amount of time it took to transfer 4% of the data over the ADSL.
